Poem #3

Moving Memories

Goodbye old world,
Old friends,
Now you are distant memories,
There is only this green car
And endless road ahead

Midway through,
We take a pitstop.
There are walls of water crashing down
And mist that dampens the air

Eventually we leave this world,
As well.
The final destination,
That’s all there’s left.
With an old green car,
And a few souvenirs,
We enter the new world
That I seem to have always known.

While this poem seems darker than it really is, it is just one of my earliest memories. I chose this because it is a reminder of some of my earliest memories, moving to Massachusetts.

WNB #3

Well, today is the day. The entire family was gathered to hear Aunt Edna's will. I hope I will get at least something that would alleviate the money struggles that I have sort of been dealing with. At this point in time, I had no idea what was in store for me. Everyone was called into what sort of looked like a boardroom but not really. The lawyer sat at the front welcoming us. He then asked us to quiet down and he began. "Before we begin", he said, "This is the finalized will of Edna Smith. Everything written in this will is final and cannot be changed." He then continued with all of the legal mumbo-jumbo. Then he paused. He looked somewhat confused. As if he had not seen this before. He looked up and promptly asked. "Before I go any further who is the youngest person present above or at the age of 18?" Everyone immediately looked at me. I then raised my hand and he asked for my name. I responded hesitantly, "Adam is my name sir". He then asked me to stand up and make my way up to him. Once I made it up to him he asked me to wait for him to finish reading the will. He picked back up saying "I Edna Smith hereby leave all my worldly possessions that being: property, savings, investments and all items of value once owned by me to..." he paused once again and looked at me. He then continued "to Adam Smith" he finally finished. He closed the folder and explained to everyone it asked for the youngest person at or above the age of 18. I was shocked. I had just become magnitudes richer. The lawyer saw me in shock and sat down once again. He opened his folder and began reading again, "The inheritance to the person named can and will only proceed if said person reveals at this time their deepest darkest secret to everyone present in the room. If this condition is not met all possessions in question are to be donated to charity or sold and have the profits donated to charity." He stopped and my heart almost sank. I was gonna throw up. I was so close to being rich yet so dar. I couldn't possibly think about sharing this secret, not in front of my parents and the rest of the family. I had only ever told aunt Edna. But this couldn't have been done to make me share my secret could it? I was being torn up from the inside out. I couldn't let all this wealth slip from my hands. I had made my decision I was gonna come clean. I opened my mouth "..." nothing came out. I tried again "Ah... umm... My..." I just couldn't do it. WNB #5

Honestly, I don’t think there is any world where privacy is fully dead. Well, not unless you think alternate dimensions are real at least. But that’s a whole other topic. I mean it sure feels like privacy is dead with net neutrality being repealed and technology becoming even more complex with microphones and cameras everywhere, but privacy is not dead. If you really wanted full privacy you wouldn’t have a smartphone or any electronic device really. When you buy these devices you are giving up a bit of your privacy and you are told what privacy the company tries to preserve. Then when you use internet services you are putting yourself in the public's eye. So in that sense, you have as much privacy as you want. You are just giving up services for that privacy. If you still want these services and privacy that is more difficult, but that doesn’t mean privacy is dead. I just mean it is harder to get to. If you really want you can encrypt data and secure everything that you want. You just need to know how to.
